Dimensional Framework
Measuring what matters for sustainable, resilient urban futures

Measuring the right indicators is key to building sustainable and resilient cities. It helps guide smarter decisions that protect the environment and improve quality of life. Together, we can create urban futures that thrive amid change.

Urban Governance and Integrity

Urban Governance and Integrity serves as the institutional backbone of the Veridian Urban Index. This pillar assesses the transparency, accountability, responsiveness, and ethical strength of a city’s governance architecture.

Urban Education, Learning Ecosystems, and Knowledge Equity

Urban Education, Learning Ecosystems, and Knowledge Equity serves as a foundational pillar of the Veridian Urban Index.

02

Business and Investment Environment

Business and Investment Environment captures the degree to which a city fosters a dynamic, inclusive, and legally secure environment for entrepreneurship, investment, and commercial innovation.

03

Smartness and Digital Readiness

Smartness and Digital Readiness assesses the extent to which a city embeds digital technologies, data-driven decision-making, and smart infrastructure into its core urban systems.

04

Cleanliness and Sanitation

Maintaining cleanliness to prevent the spread of diseases. Building a healthier tomorrow with sanitation awareness.

05

Conflict Risk and Early Warning

Conflict Management and Early Warning addresses one of the most urgent challenges of 21st-century urbanization—anticipating and preventing violent conflict in cities.

06

Civic Resilience and Social Cohesion

Civic resilience is the capacity of a city's people, institutions, and networks to absorb shocks—social, political, economic, or environmental—while preserving unity, trust, and functionality.

07

Housing and Land Security

Housing and land security form the structural bedrock of equitable urban development.

08

Environmental Hazards and Urban Safety

Environmental Hazards and Urban Safety interrogates the structural interdependencies between ecological risk, urban design, and public vulnerability.

09

Public Health, Inclusion, and Wellbeing

Public Health, Inclusion, and Wellbeing explores the capacity of urban systems to safeguard human health as a fundamental dimension of city resilience and inclusive development.

10

Infrastructure, Mobility, and Service Delivery

nfrastructure, Mobility, and Service Delivery interrogates the foundational systems that enable a city to function as a coherent, equitable, and resilient organism.

11

Green Infrastructure, Forests, and Urban Ecology

Green Infrastructure, Forests, and Urban Ecologyaddresses the ecological foundations of urban sustainability, framing nature not as an externality but as an embedded, co-evolving system within the urban fabric.

12

Employment and Workforce Development

As cities experience unprecedented population growth—particularly across Africa, Asia, and Latin America—the demand for equitable, sustainable, and dignified employment has become a defining challenge of 21st-century urban governance.

13

Cultural Heritage, Identity, and Narrative Power

As cities expand, they do so not on empty land but atop layers of memory, identity, and cultural meaning. Urbanization often collides with sacred spaces, oral traditions, and heritage landscapes—riskingerasure when growth ignores the symbolic fabric of place.

Justification: Selected Score: 4

The city maintains a dedicated “Urban Governance Portal” where all municipal acts, land-use regulations, environmental bylaws, and citizen charters are published. These documents are updated quarterly and available in PDF and HTML formats. In addition, recent amendments and council resolutions are uploaded within 15 days of approval. This ensures that residents and stakeholders can easily access the most current legal framework at any time.

Scoring guide:

4 - Comprehensive legal framework is available online and in multiple formats.
3 - Legal framework is available but not regularly updated.
2 - Legal documents are available only upon request.
1 - Partial legal framework available or outdated.
0 - No accessible legal framework.

Justification: Selected Score: 2

The city publishes an annual budget summary through its Finance Department webpage and press releases. The report includes total allocations for major sectors (infrastructure, sanitation, health, and education), but does not provide department-wise or project-level line items. Citizens can request detailed data under the Right to Information (RTI) mechanism, but it’s not publicly posted by default.

Scoring guide:

3 - Budgets are published but not fully disaggregated.
